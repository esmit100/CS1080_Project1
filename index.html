Know the risk. Make better decisions.

Avalanches are rare, but consequences can be huge. This site is a beginner-friendly overview of avalanche basics, simple planning habits, and a rescue workflow.

  • Understand avalanche problems and red flags

⚠️ This is educational, not professional instruction. Always take a certified course and follow your local avalanche forecast.

Quick “Before You Go” Check

  1. Read today’s avalanche forecast
  2. Choose terrain that matches the danger rating
  3. Do a beacon check with your group
  4. Bring: beacon, shovel, probe (and know how to use them)

Pick a conservative “default plan” before you leave, and only upgrade it if conditions look good — not the other way around.
